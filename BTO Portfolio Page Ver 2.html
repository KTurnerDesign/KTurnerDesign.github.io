BTO

After __ years, the BTO's brand was in need of a refresh. This was done through an update to the brand image and website.
The Challenge
Branding
Founded in 1932, the BTO has seen a lot of change over the years. Although rebranded around 2010, this look was starting to show its age, as well as not appealing to a younger market that it would need to survive. This project was intended to help with that issue.


Website Design
Much of the layout of the original website would still need to be carried over. This was because the BTO still uses these to communicate with their volunteers as well as being a reliable source of information about the environment. This meant it would be a facelist rather than starting from scratch.
Research
Brand Research
The initial step was to take a look at what similar brands have already done as well as some that visually represent the direct the BTO might want to move in.






Internal research
I also looked at the visual identity of the BTO and the current website to better understand the important elements for this rebrand.




Design
With a better understanding of the original brand and their competitors, I set out creating the visual identity that would be the framework for the rest of the design.


Brand Colours
I chose XX and XX to represent the brand as these were bright, fun and appealing to a younger audience. This would also help to move away from the dull colours originally in place.






Typography
XX was chosen for the titles as this tied well with (condensed font) is creating a modern, sleek look. XX was paired with this as the body text due to have open, round characters that worked well with the similar elements in (condensed font).
